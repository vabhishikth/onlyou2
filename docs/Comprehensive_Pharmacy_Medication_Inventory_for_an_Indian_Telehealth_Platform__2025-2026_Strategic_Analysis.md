# Comprehensive pharmacy medication inventory for an Indian telehealth platform

India's generic pharmaceutical market — the world's largest by volume — offers extraordinary breadth across all five telehealth verticals covered here. **This inventory catalogs 400+ products across hair loss, erectile dysfunction, premature ejaculation, weight management, and PCOS**, spanning prescription drugs, adjuncts, supplements, topicals, and devices. Every medication listed is available through Indian pharmaceutical supply chains with Indian brand names, manufacturers, and approximate MRP pricing in INR. Cold chain requirements apply only to injectable GLP-1 agonists and select progesterone formulations; all other products store at room temperature below 25–30°C.

Three cross-cutting facts shape inventory planning. First, **India uniquely offers combination products unavailable in Western markets** — topical Minoxidil+Finasteride solutions, Dapoxetine+PDE5 inhibitor tablets, and multi-ingredient cosmeceutical serums. Second, the March 2026 semaglutide patent expiry has unleashed 40+ generic manufacturers, collapsing prices from ₹16,400/month to as low as ₹1,250/month. Third, virtually all prescription medications in this inventory fall under Schedule H, with only isotretinoin requiring Schedule H1 enhanced record-keeping.

**Tirzepatide** (dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist) — the most effective weight loss drug available, showing up to **22% body weight loss** in clinical trials. ### Oral weight management drugs

**Orlistat** (lipase inhibitor) — blocks ~30% dietary fat absorption. Obelit (Intas), Orlean (Eris), Xenical. 120mg capsules three times daily with meals. Schedule H. ₹150–500/strip. Room temperature. Modest weight loss (3–5%) but reliable and affordable.

**Metformin** (off-label for weight/insulin resistance) — 249+ brands in India. Glycomet (USV), Glyciphage (Franco-Indian), Okamet (Cipla). 500–1000mg doses. ₹15–93/strip. The most affordable metabolic drug available, excellent for PCOS-related weight gain.

**Topiramate** (off-label for weight loss) — anticonvulsant causing 5–7% weight loss. Topamac (J&J ₹100/strip), Topaz (Intas), Topirol. 25–100mg strengths. Very affordable at ₹94–300/strip. **Significant cognitive side effects and teratogenicity risk.**

### Drugs NOT available in India

**Phentermine** — not marketed or approved in India. Classified internationally as Schedule IV controlled substance. **Setmelanotide** (IMCIVREE) — not approved, orphan drug for rare genetic obesity only. **Naltrexone/Bupropion fixed combination** (Contrave) — not officially DCGI-approved as a combination; individual components available separately (4 naltrexone brands, 13 bupropion brands).

**Banned weight-loss drugs in India**: Sibutramine, R-Sibutramine, Rimonabant, Dexfenfluramine, Phenylpropanolamine.

### Adjunct medications for GLP-1 side effects

Anti-nausea (for GLP-1 side effects): **Ondansetron** — Emeset (Cipla), Vomikind (Mankind); **Domperidone** — Domstal (Torrent). Anti-acid: **Pantoprazole** — Pan (Alkem), Pan-D; **Omeprazole** — Omez (Dr. Reddy's). Laxatives for constipation. **Ursodeoxycholic acid** for gallstone prevention during rapid weight loss.

### Weight management supplements

Critical with Orlistat (which blocks fat-soluble vitamin absorption): fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K separately. **Fiber supplements**: Isabgol/Psyllium husk (very popular in India, Sat Isabgol brands ₹50–200). Probiotics: Vizylac, Darolac, Econorm. Green tea extract, Garcinia cambogia, L-Carnitine, CLA, Chromium picolinate supplements available from HealthKart, Carbamide Forte, and other Indian brands. Protein supplements (MuscleBlaze, MyProtein India).

### Cold chain summary for this vertical
**Requires cold chain (2–8°C):** All injectable semaglutide, liraglutide, and tirzepatide. **Room temperature:** Oral semaglutide (Rybelsus), orlistat, metformin, topiramate, all supplements.

## Conclusion

This inventory reflects a market where **affordability and breadth of choice** are India's defining advantages. Generic competition has pushed prices to a fraction of global averages across every vertical — finasteride at ₹1/tablet, dapoxetine at ₹5/tablet, metformin at ₹1.5/tablet. The March 2026 semaglutide patent expiry is the most transformative recent event, making the world's most effective weight loss injectable accessible at ₹1,250/month versus ₹16,400 branded.

For telehealth platform inventory planning, three categories deserve priority stocking. First, the **combination topicals and tablets unique to India** (Minoxidil+Finasteride, Dapoxetine+PDE5 inhibitors) which will be prescribed most frequently. Second, the **generic GLP-1 agonists** from established manufacturers (Dr. Reddy's, Zydus, Natco) which represent the highest-value growth opportunity but require cold chain logistics. Third, **PCOS supplement combinations** (Myo-inositol+DCI+Folic acid+Vitamin D bundles) which offer high prescribing frequency with simpler logistics. Schedule H compliance infrastructure is essential for the entire prescription inventory, with isotretinoin requiring the additional Schedule H1 register. All medications except injectable GLP-1s and select progesterone formulations store at standard room temperature, simplifying warehouse requirements across the majority of SKUs.
